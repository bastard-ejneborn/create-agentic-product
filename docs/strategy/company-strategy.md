## Vision & Mission

**Position**: Sweden's strongest burger brand — winning on taste, service quality, raw ingredient quality, restaurant feel, and value perception.

**Long-term financial target**: 
- Revenue per restaurant: 11.2 MSEK
- EBITDA per restaurant: 2.6 MSEK  
- EBITDA margin: 23.5%

**Current state (2024)**:
- Revenue per restaurant: 9.3-9.5 MSEK
- EBITDA margin: 15.0-16.2%
- Significant gap to close vs. peak (2019: 11.9 MSEK, 23.5%)

## Strategic Priorities (2025, ranked)

### Overarching Theme: Cashflow & EBITDA
Not building new capabilities from scratch anymore — harvesting the results of Year 1's foundation work.

### 1. Go Digital — From Launch to Optimization
**Goal**: Increase revenue through digital channels
- Increase conversion to digital ordering (app, kiosks)
- Drive loyalty program growth (members and visit frequency)
- A/B test digital sales channels to optimize revenue
- Context: 54% of 16-29 year olds prefer express kiosks, 24% prefer app. Core demographic aligns with digital push.

### 2. Street Food Innovators — Chicken & Campaigns
**Goal**: Broaden the menu to drive traffic and average check
- **Chicken launch** — major priority, backed by consumer data (30% want to try chicken)
- **Shake improvements**
- **Campaign framework**: Clear distinction between traffic-driving campaigns (more visits) and check-driving campaigns (more per visit)
- Context: 71% ate beef burger last time, but forward-looking demand favors chicken and vegetarian.

### 3. Dig Where We Stand — Squeeze More From Existing Restaurants
**Goal**: Maximize revenue from current locations
- Extended opening hours / daypart expansion
- Local marketing (drive awareness at neighborhood level)
- Professionalized pricing (new for 2025 — carried over from Year 1)
- Cost savings
- **NEW**: B2B sales (catering, corporate events)
- **NEW**: Portfolio management (open/close restaurants strategically)

### 4. Foundation Improvements — Next Maturity Phase
**Goal**: Maintain operational excellence while scaling complexity
- **NEW**: Staff engagement and pride
- **NEW**: Competence development at restaurant manager level
- **NEW**: Maintain guest experience despite increased operational complexity (more channels, broader menu, more systems)
- Year 1 foundations all delivered ✅ (training, food safety, scheduling, reporting, org structure, IT environment, governance, portfolio, maintenance)

## Constraints & Non-Negotiables

- **Consumer price sensitivity is at historic highs**: 53% report weakened willingness to pay for restaurant visits (up from 5% in 2022). Every price increase must be carefully considered.
- **Operational complexity increasing**: More channels (app, kiosk, delivery, counter), broader menu (chicken, vegan, topped fries), more systems. Must not degrade guest experience.
- **Profitability first**: No growth for growth's sake. Cashflow and EBITDA are the measures that matter.
- **Quality is the moat**: BB wins on taste, service, ingredient quality, restaurant feel. This must not be compromised for efficiency.
